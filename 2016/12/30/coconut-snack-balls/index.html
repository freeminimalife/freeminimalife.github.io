4-Ingredient Coconut Snack Balls

Super Simple and Healthy Coconut Snack Balls Recipe

Recently I’ve been trying to cut down my sugar intake, as I’m planning on starting this new year eating healthier and reducing my overall sugar consumption. I was practicing with some of the ingredients I have at home, and this very delicious snacking balls came as a result. I really don’t like to make super complicated stuff with a bunch of crazy super expensive ingredients.

I like simple stuff.

Those recipes that take a lot of time and also quite some money, are definitely not what I’m looking for: I have a tight schedule and budget, but at the same time I want to be able to eat healthy and delicious.

This is an incredibly easy recipe with just 4 ingredients: Coconut flour, coconut oil, quark and chia seeds, without the addition of any type of sugar, and just with very simple and natural whole foods that will help you go through your day without feeling tired, hungry and avoiding reaching the sugary stuff that surrounds you. It will take no more than 5 minutes and will provide you with all the healthy fats and protein needed to curb your hunger.

Note: The ingredients used for this recipe are naturally low in sugar, and even without adding any sweeteners, the natural aroma in coconuts adds a very nice flavor.



Coconut is my favorite food in the world!!! Well… At least is in my top 5, for sure! It’s a hard choice :D

To prepare these nice balls, the ingredients and measurements that you would need for 2 portions - that’s around 6 small balls - are the following:

Ingredients

  • 3 tsp coconut oil.
  • 3 tbsps 40% quark (or full fat Greek yogurt).
  • 5 tbsp coconut flour (or shredded coconut).
  • 1 tsp chia seeds.
    Note: You can try adding some stevia, but I don’t find it particularly necessary. That’s up to your taste!

Preparation

  1. Mix the coconut oil with the quark, 4 tablespoons of coconut flour and the chia seeds.
  2. When everything is mixed together proceed to make small balls, around 6.
  3. After having the small coconut balls formed, cover them with the remaining coconut flour.
  4. You can now proceed to refrigerate them if you’re planning on eating them later, or just have them right away! They will be firm enough to be eaten just after preparing them.

Hope you enjoy this coconut snack balls recipe!
